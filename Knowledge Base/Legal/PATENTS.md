# PATENTS — Defensive Publication and Patent Policy

Version 1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the patent and defensive publication strategy for the CoSyn project.

The goal is to:

- preserve freedom to operate
- prevent hostile patent claims on core governance concepts
- protect the long‑term evolution of the CoSyn system

## Defensive Publication

Certain architectural concepts in CoSyn may be intentionally disclosed in public repositories or documentation in order to establish prior art.

Defensive publication helps prevent third parties from obtaining patents on core governance mechanisms already developed by this project.

Examples may include:

- governance gate architectures
- reasoning pipeline governance methods
- telemetry‑based reasoning audit systems
- deterministic reasoning enforcement structures

## Patent Candidates

Some components may qualify for patent protection, including but not limited to:

- runtime governance pipelines
- structured reasoning gate systems
- deterministic AI governance architectures
- governance‑enforced AI runtime environments

The project maintainer may pursue patent filings where appropriate.

Public repository publication does not waive patent rights unless explicitly stated.

## Contributor Notice

By contributing to this repository, contributors acknowledge that:

- their contributions may be included in defensive publications
- their contributions may be incorporated into patent applications

Contributors grant the project maintainer the right to include contributions in such filings where legally permitted.

## No Patent License Granted

Unless explicitly stated in a license agreement, this repository does not grant any patent license for commercial use of patented technologies.

Commercial rights may be granted through a separate commercial license agreement.
